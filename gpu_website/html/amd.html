RX 460

One of the cheaper graphics cards that compromises power for
affordability. It comes in two variations, the 2 GB and the
4 GB. Since games already use on average 4 GB of VRAM the 2GB
will severely limit the ability to get the most out of the graphics
of games.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 896
Memory Speed: 2/4 GB
Boost Clock: 1200 MHz


RX 470

The 470 provides great performance but the problem comes from its
price. It is closely priced near the RX 480 which is far better.
It may be better to just spend a few more bucks on getting a 480
than the 470 because of how closely they are priced.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 2048
Memory Speed: 4 GB
Boost Clock: 1206 MHz


RX 480

The most powerful card in the 400 series. It makes it a great card
to play at 1080p resolution and is capable of VR. With this performance
comes a relative low power draw at just 150W. It comes in two version,
the 4 GB and the 8GB. The 8GB would be the best one to get because of
the extra VRAM that can be used to play at higher resolutions.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 2304
Memory Speed: 4/8 GB
Boost Clock: 1080 MHz



RX 550

The 550 is not good for those that want good graphics in every game.
This card will do great for esports games such as CSGO, League of Legends,
Rocket League etc. If your looking to play a game like Witcher 3 and
want 60 frames than this is not for you. It’s a cheap card and because
of that it can’t run games like Witcher 3 at decent frames.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 512
Memory Speed: 2 GB
Boost Clock: 1183 GHz


RX 560

RX 560 is similar to Nvidia’s 1050 GPU. It does perform a bit worse
than the 1050 and is around the same price. It does not make much
sense to go with RX 560 instead of the 1050 unless you are an AMD
fanboy. If the 560 was cheaper it would be able to compete with the
1050 but unfortunately Nvidia provides a better card at the same price.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 896/1024
Memory Speed: 2/4 GB
Boost Clock: 1257 GHz


RX 570

Very similar to the RX 470 but a bit faster by about 5 to 10%.
Its not a huge upgrade if you are looking to upgrade from the 470.
It is a disappointment because of the lack of performance. It is not
that much more expensive than the 470 so it is worth buying over the
470 but not worth upgrading.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 2048
Memory Speed: 4 GB
Boost Clock: 1244 MHz


RX 580

The RX 580 has a huge VRAM. Its 8GB which is designed towards
playing at 1440p. It’s a waste of money to get this card for
1080p because you will use no where near 8GB of VRAM if you play
at a resolution lower than 1440p. It can’t play 1440p at max setting,
but it is a decent card for playing at a higher resolution.

GPU Architecture: GCN 4
Cores: 2304
Memory Speed: 8 GB
Boost Clock: 1340 MHz
